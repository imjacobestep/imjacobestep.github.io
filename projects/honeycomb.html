Honeycomb

A capstone for grad school, completed in 2 quarters and sponsored by Mary's Place and Starbucks.
My roles: UX Researcher/Designer, Front-End Engineer

Problem:

Mary's Place's Diversion team lacked organized info on homelessness resources to match clients with. This put a strain on shelter capacity.

Process:

Research: We conducted interviews, contextual inquiries, cognitive walkthroughs, and co-design activities with staff. We created 3 high-fidelity prototypes and conducted 2 rounds of user testing.

Development: We split development into 5 week-long sprints, using Flutter for iOS. After each sprint, we tested any new changes. Once feature-complete, we stress-tested with batch uploads/deletes and over 130 local resources.

Result:

Honeycomb gives Diversion Specialists a reliable source of resource info, a nearby map view, search and filtering, and client-resource matching and sharing tools. Mary's Place plans to adopt Honeycomb internally in early 2023, with hopes to expand it in the future.
